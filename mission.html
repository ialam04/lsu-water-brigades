Our Mission

Dedicated to ensuring clean water access for all communities

Our Purpose

Mission Statement

The LSU Water Brigades is committed to addressing water accessibility challenges through community service, advocacy, and sustainable solutions. We strive to create lasting impact by partnering with local and global communities to ensure everyone has access to clean, safe water.

Our organization believes that access to clean water is a fundamental human right, and we work tirelessly to make this vision a reality through education, direct action, and collaborative partnerships.

Our Vision

We envision a world where every person has reliable access to clean water, where communities are empowered with the knowledge and tools to maintain their water systems, and where young leaders are equipped to address global water challenges.

Through our work at LSU and beyond, we aim to create a generation of water advocates who will continue this mission long after their university years.

Our Core Values

The principles that guide everything we do

Equity

We believe that access to clean water is a fundamental human right that should be available to all people, regardless of their location, economic status, or background.

Sustainability

We focus on creating long-term, environmentally responsible solutions that communities can maintain and benefit from for generations to come.

Community

We work in partnership with communities, respecting local knowledge and ensuring that our projects meet actual needs and priorities.

Education

We believe in the power of education to create lasting change, both in the communities we serve and among our student volunteers.

Innovation

We embrace creative and innovative approaches to solving water challenges, always looking for new and better ways to make an impact.

Integrity

We operate with transparency, accountability, and ethical practices in all our work and partnerships.

Our Story

How LSU Water Brigades came to be

Our Beginning

LSU Water Brigades was founded by a group of passionate LSU students who recognized the urgent need to address water accessibility issues both in Louisiana and around the world. What started as a small group of dedicated volunteers has grown into a robust organization making meaningful impact in communities.

Our Growth

Over the years, we have expanded our reach and refined our approach. We've learned that effective water advocacy requires not just good intentions, but also collaboration, cultural sensitivity, and sustainable planning. Today, we continue to evolve our methods while staying true to our core mission.

Our Focus Areas

The key areas where we make our greatest impact

Local Environmental Protection

We protect and restore local water sources through cleanup initiatives, conservation projects, and advocacy for environmental policies that safeguard our community's water resources.

  • LSU Lakes restoration and cleanup
  • Stormwater management advocacy
  • Water quality monitoring
  • Community education programs

Community Development

We partner with organizations to build sustainable infrastructure that ensures communities have access to clean water and proper sanitation facilities.

  • Habitat for Humanity partnerships
  • Infrastructure development support
  • Community capacity building
  • Long-term sustainability planning

Direct Service Missions

Our brigade missions provide direct support to communities in need, delivering both immediate assistance and long-term solutions for water access challenges.

  • Water system installation and repair
  • Emergency water relief
  • Technical training and support
  • Community needs assessment

Education & Advocacy

We raise awareness about water issues and advocate for policies that promote water justice and accessibility at local, national, and international levels.

  • Campus awareness campaigns
  • Policy advocacy and research
  • Educational workshops and seminars
  • Partnership with other advocacy groups

Our Goals

What we're working towards in the coming years

Short-term Goals (2025-2026)

  • Expand our local service projects to include more community partners
  • Increase student engagement and volunteer participation by 50%
  • Launch a comprehensive water education program for LSU students
  • Establish partnerships with additional environmental organizations
  • Complete at least 4 major community service projects

Long-term Vision (2027-2030)

  • Become a leading campus organization for water advocacy in the region
  • Establish international partnership programs for global water projects
  • Create a sustainable funding model for ongoing operations
  • Develop an alumni network that continues water advocacy work
  • Launch innovative water technology research initiatives
